I heard to Elder Wirthlin's talk entitled *Come What May, and Love it*

Elder Joseph B. Wirthlin received from his mother the counsel *Come What May, and Love it*. The way that we react to adversity can be a major factor in how happy and succesful we can be. From his own experience, he teaches some attitudes that helped him through times of test and trial:

- Learn to Laugh. The next time you're tempted to groan try laughing instead :)

- Seek For the Eternal. The difficult experiences

  + Stretch our understanding
  + Build our character
  + Increase our compasion for others, as Jesus feels comasiion on us
    because of His suffering in Gethsemany.

- Understand the Principle of Compensation. The Lord compensates the faithful
  for every loss. Every tear of grief will be returned hundredfold in tears
  of rejoice and gratitude.

- Trust the Lord, do your best, and leave the rest to Him

QUESTION: What have you learned from your own setbacks?

I've learned that the Lord keeps His promises. Then, when things don't go as
expected, don't blame myself or others, just review the process and perform
corrections. Feeling guilty or powerless does not help to learn and grow;
instead, feeling responsible, remembering that I am a child of God and that
He will be at my right and at my left, and then *acting* on my knowledge and
the promptings of the Spirit will lead me to *green pastures*.
